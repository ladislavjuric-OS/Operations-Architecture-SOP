## Purpose

The MVL (Minimum Viable Launch) Gate Protocol is a structured decision framework that prevents premature launches, premature scaling, and premature spend.

It translates the Forensic Audit verdict into **actionable gates** â€” specific conditions that must be met before proceeding. Each gate has a clear owner, deadline, and measurable pass/fail criteria.

This protocol is used when the audit produces a **Conditional GO** â€” the most common (and most dangerous) verdict, because it feels like permission but is actually a structured challenge.

---

## Core Principle: Gates, Not Hopes

Most projects fail not because the idea was bad, but because the team:
- Launched before the audience was ready
- Spent before the math was validated
- Scaled before the foundation could hold

MVL Gates force a binary answer at each checkpoint: **PASS or FAIL.**
No "we'll figure it out." No "it should be fine." Numbers or nothing.

## The Gate Structure

### Gate 0: Audit Complete
**Trigger:** Forensic Audit delivered with scored modules
**Pass criteria:**
- All 8 modules scored
- Overall readiness score calculated
- Verdict issued (GO / Conditional GO / NO-GO)
- Critical blockers identified and documented

**If NO-GO:** Stop. Restructure or pivot. No gates needed â€” the answer is clear.
**If GO:** Proceed to execution. Gates are advisory checkpoints.
**If Conditional GO:** Gates become mandatory. No gate skip = no launch.
