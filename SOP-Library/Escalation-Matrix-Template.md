# Escalation Matrix Template

> "If the Founder/Owner is the answer to a question that happens more than once a week, it needs a Level 2 SOP or a Level 1 automation. Not another conversation."

---

## Purpose

Defines which decisions require the Founder/Owner or senior operator, and which can be delegated or systematised.

Goal: eliminate the Founder/Owner as the default answer to every operational question.

---

## The Four Levels

### Level 1 — Systemised (no human needed)

- Re-order triggers below par level
- Status updates from confirmed data sources
- Automated reporting at fixed intervals
- Routine dispatch confirmations within agreed parameters

### Level 2 — Delegated (team decides)

- Allocation of stock to orders within agreed rules
- PO confirmation within approved supplier and budget range
- Standard customer comms within SOP
- Routine exception handling covered by existing policy

### Level 3 — Escalate to Ops Lead

- Allocation conflict: demand exceeds available stock
- Supplier delay beyond agreed buffer
- Customer complaint outside SOP scope
- Cross-department conflict on priorities

### Level 4 — Founder/Owner decision required

- New supplier onboarding
- Pricing change or discount approval above threshold
- Out-of-scope customer resolution above threshold
- Any decision that sets a new precedent
- Resource allocation across competing priorities

---

## How to Build This

1. Run through your last 30 days of decisions
2. Categorise each into Level 1–4
3. If 80%+ are Level 3–4 going to the Founder/Owner: you have a delegation problem, not a staffing problem
4. Build SOPs and decision trees for everything in Level 2
5. Identify automation candidates in Level 1
6. Document, share, and review monthly

---

## Output Format

One written document. Reviewed and signed off by the Founder/Owner.
Not a flowchart. Not a RACI matrix. A clear list of what needs them and what does not.

---

## System: Before → After

| Dimension | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Decision routing | Everything goes to Founder/Owner | Clear level for every decision type |
| Team confidence | Team asks before acting | Team acts within defined boundaries |
| Founder/Owner time | Consumed by Level 1-2 decisions | Reserved for Level 3-4 only |
| Accountability | Unclear who owns what | Every decision level has an owner |
| Exceptions | Handled ad hoc, inconsistently | Covered by SOP or escalation protocol |
| New team onboarding | Tribal knowledge of when to ask | Written framework, learnable day one |
| Operations at scale | Founder/Owner bottleneck grows with headcount | System scales without the bottleneck |

---

## Diagnostic

Use this to assess your current escalation health:

| Question | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Can your team handle a full day without contacting you? | ✅ Delegated | ❌ Build Level 2 SOPs |
| Do you have written rules for stock allocation? | ✅ Level 2 covered | ❌ Document now |
| Can someone else approve a standard supplier PO? | ✅ Delegated | ❌ Define approval rules |
| Do you know which decisions only you can make? | ✅ Level 4 defined | ❌ Define boundaries |
| Is your team clear on what “escalate” means? | ✅ Protocol exists | ❌ Write the protocol |

**Score 4–5:** Functional escalation system. Tune and automate.
**Score 2–3:** Partial. Fill the gaps in Level 2.
**Score 0–1:** Rebuild from scratch with this framework.
