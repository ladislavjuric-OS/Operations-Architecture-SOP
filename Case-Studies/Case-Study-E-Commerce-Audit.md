## The Situation

A California-based fashion brand approached us to launch on Indiegogo. On paper, the brand looked promising:
- 140K+ Instagram followers
- Strong visual identity
- Physical product ready for crowdfunding

The founder's assumption: "We have the audience. We just need someone to run the campaign."

**Our assumption: Nothing is real until the numbers say so.**

## The Verdict

**Initial verdict: NO-GO.**

140K Instagram followers with zero email subscribers = zero launch infrastructure. In crowdfunding, social followers convert at ~0.5-1%. Email subscribers convert at ~5-15%. Without an email list, this campaign had a ~75% probability of failure.

**But we didn't walk away.**

We identified a viable path: a **Conditional GO** with a 6-8 week MVL (Minimum Viable Launch) protocol.

## The Lesson

This case demonstrates the core Juric OS philosophy:

1. **Diagnosis before prescription.** The founder thought the problem was "running a campaign." The actual problem was "zero launch infrastructure."
2. **NO-GO is a valid â€” and valuable â€” answer.** Saving a founder from a premature launch is worth more than the launch fee.
3. **Conditional GO creates a path.** Instead of binary "do it / don't do it," we engineer the conditions for success and let the founder decide if they'll do the work.
4. **Numbers decide.** Not excitement, not follower counts, not "gut feeling." The 100-point score is the arbiter.
